Bechamel Sauce Recipe
====================
Bechamel sauce, also known as white sauce, is a classic French sauce made from a roux of butter and flour, combined with milk. It serves as a base for many other sauces and dishes. Here's a simple recipe to make bechamel sauce at home.
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 cups milk
- Salt and pepper to taste
- A pinch of nutmeg (optional)
Instructions:
1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat until it is foamy.
2. Add the flour to the melted butter and whisk continuously for about 1-2 minutes until the mixture is smooth and has a slightly nutty aroma. This is called a roux.
3. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Continue to whisk until the mixture is smooth.
4. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, stirring frequently. The sauce will thicken as it heats. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
5. Once the sauce has thickened, remove it from the heat. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Stir well to combine.
6. Your bechamel sauce is now ready to use! You can use it as a base for other sauces, such as cheese sauce or mushroom sauce, or as a component in dishes like lasagna, macaroni and cheese, or gratins.
Enjoy your homemade bechamel sauce!
